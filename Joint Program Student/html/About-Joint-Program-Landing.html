Frequently Asked Questions

Eligibility • English • Credit transfer • Tuition • Timeline • Visa

Who is a good fit for 1+3 vs 2+2?
1+3 suits students who want to go overseas sooner; 2+2 suits those optimizing total cost and needing more time for English preparation.
What English level is required?
Depends on university/major. Typical: IELTS 6.0–6.5 or TOEFL iBT 79–85. Bridging options may be available.
Will my credits be accepted?
Yes. We support course mapping and articulation so your transferable credits are evaluated properly.
How much does it cost? Any scholarships?
Estimate: ~$4k/year in Vietnam; ~$25k–$35k/year in the U.S. Many partners offer merit/transfer scholarships.
What is the usual application timeline?
Plan 6–12 months ahead for course mapping, English tests, document preparation, and visa filing.
What documents are needed?
Transcripts, course syllabi (for credit evaluation), English test score or proof, passport, financial documents, and statement of purpose.
How does the visa process work?
We guide I-20/DS‑160 prep, interview tips, and paperwork review. Start early to avoid delays.
What support will I receive after transfer?
Advising on course registration, housing recommendations, arrival orientation, and on‑campus resources.
