What is 1+3 Program?

The 1+3 Transfer Program allows students to complete their first year of university studies in Vietnam, then transfer to complete the remaining 3 years at a partner university abroad.

Year 1 (Vietnam)

Foundation studies, English prep, and general education at partner campus

Years 2-4 (Overseas)

Continue major courses at an international university and graduate with a full degree

Credit Transfer

~30 credits from VN year are evaluated and transferred according to articulation

Why Choose 1+3 Program?

Cost Effective

Save significantly on first-year tuition and living costs

Gradual Transition

Adjust to international education while staying close to family

Language Preparation

Intensive English training before going abroad

Support Network

Travel with Vietnamese classmates; buddy system in the first semester abroad

Entry Flexibility

Suitable for students building GPA/English to meet direct-entry thresholds

Scholarships

Eligible for transfer/merit scholarships at many partner universities

Who Should Choose 1+3?

Students wanting early exposure abroad while minimizing first-year costs

Learners who prefer a gradual academic and cultural transition

Students planning to build English and GPA in Vietnam before overseas study

Support & Services

Course mapping & articulation advising

Visa guidance and document review

Housing recommendations and arrival orientation

What is 2+2 Program?

The 2+2 Transfer Program allows students to complete their 2 year of university studies in Vietnam, then transfer to complete the remaining 2 years at a partner university abroad.

Year 1-2 (Vietnam)

Foundation studies, English prep, and general education at partner campus

Years 3-4 (Overseas)

Continue major courses at an international university and graduate with a full degree

Credit Transfer

~50 credits from VN year are evaluated and transferred according to articulation

Why Choose 2+2 Program?

Maximum Savings

Save up to 50% of total education costs

Strong Foundation

Build solid academic base before going abroad

More Preparation Time

Extra year to improve English and adapt to international curriculum

Career Ready

Internship opportunities in Vietnam before studying abroad

Family Support

Stay close to family for two years while building independence

Transfer Scholarships

Eligible for merit scholarships at partner universities

Who Should Choose 2+2?

Students seeking maximum cost savings

Frequently Asked Questions

Eligibility • English • Credit transfer • Tuition • Timeline • Visa

Who is a good fit for 1+3 vs 2+2?
1+3 suits students who want to go overseas sooner; 2+2 suits those optimizing total cost and needing more time for English preparation.
What English level is required?
Depends on university/major. Typical: IELTS 6.0–6.5 or TOEFL iBT 79–85. Bridging options may be available.
Will my credits be accepted?
Yes. We support course mapping and articulation so your transferable credits are evaluated properly.
How much does it cost? Any scholarships?
Estimate: ~$4k/year in Vietnam; ~$25k–$35k/year in the U.S. Many partners offer merit/transfer scholarships.
What is the usual application timeline?
Plan 6–12 months ahead for course mapping, English tests, document preparation, and visa filing.
What documents are needed?
Transcripts, course syllabi (for credit evaluation), English test score or proof, passport, financial documents, and statement of purpose.
How does the visa process work?
We guide I-20/DS‑160 prep, interview tips, and paperwork review. Start early to avoid delays.
What support will I receive after transfer?
Advising on course registration, housing recommendations, arrival orientation, and on‑campus resources.
