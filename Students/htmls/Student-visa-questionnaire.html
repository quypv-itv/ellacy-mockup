MOST FREQUENTLY USED VISA QUESTIONNAIRES FOR F1 VISA INTERVIEW

At the Student Visa Interview, The Consular Office often ask you many questions mainly within the following:

1. Good morning! Please introduce yourself.

Suggested Answer: Good morning. My name is [Name]. I'm from [City, Vietnam]. I just graduated from [school/high school] and plan to study in the U.S. for my bachelor's degree.

2. Why do you want to study in the US?

Suggested Answer: I want to study in the US because of its world-class education system and the opportunity to gain international exposure. The program I'm applying to at [University Name] is particularly strong in [specific field] and aligns perfectly with my career goals.

3. Have you ever been granted a U.S. visa?

Suggested Answer: No, I have never been granted a U.S. visa. (Or: Yes, I had a B1/B2 visa in 2022 for tourism.)

4. Have you ever been rejected a U.S. visa?

Suggested Answer: No, I have never been refused a U.S. visa.

5. What is the purpose of your trip?

Suggested Answer: To pursue my [bachelor's/master's] degree in [major] at [school name].

6. Why don't you choose another country to study?

Suggested Answer: The U.S. offers the program and research opportunities that fit my career goals. Other countries don't provide the same curriculum.

7. Why don't you choose another school to study?

Suggested Answer: This school offers the exact major, scholarships, and academic environment I need. I also like its location and campus size.

8. Why do you choose this school to study?

Suggested Answer: It matches my major, has strong professors in [field], and provides good internship opportunities.

9. How long do you want to study in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: My program is four years, so I will stay about four years.

10. What will you study in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I will study [major] as a full-time student.

11. What will you major in when you study university?

Suggested Answer: My major is [major].

12. Why do you choose that major?

Suggested Answer: I'm interested in [short reasonâ€”e.g., technology solving real problems, finance for global business]. It matches my career plan in Vietnam.

14. What's your father's/mother's job?

Suggested Answer: My father is a [job title]. My mother is a [job title].

15. How much do your parents earn a month? How much is your family monthly income?

Suggested Answer: My family earns about [amount] per month, enough to cover all my study costs.

16. Who will pay for your study in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: My parents will fully sponsor my education and living expenses.

17. Do your parents have a saving book or banking account? How much?

Suggested Answer: Yes, my parents have savings of about [amount] USD in their bank account.

18. Will you return to Vietnam when you finish studying?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I will return to Vietnam after graduation to work in [industry] and be near my family.

19. How can you prove that you will return to Vietnam?

Suggested Answer: My family, property, and career plan are all in Vietnam. I have no intention to immigrate.

20. What will you do after you finish your study in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I'll come back to Vietnam to start my career in [field].

21. How long do you intend to stay in the U.S. after you finish your study?

Suggested Answer: I will return home as soon as I finish my program.

22. Do you intend to work in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: No, I do not plan to work in the U.S. except possible on-campus jobs allowed by F-1 regulations.

22b. If you are offered a good job with high salary, will you agree to work?

Suggested Answer: I appreciate the idea, but my plan is to return to Vietnam. I will not stay long-term.

23. What makes me should grant you a visa according to you?

Suggested Answer: I'm a serious student with a clear plan, strong finances, and strong ties to Vietnam. I meet all F-1 requirements.

24. Why do you think I should give you a visa?

Suggested Answer: Because I have a well-prepared academic plan, solid financial support, and a clear intention to return to Vietnam.

25. What will you do if I say that you are not qualified for a visa?

Suggested Answer: I would ask for feedback, fix any issues, and re-apply after correcting them.

26. Do you have any relatives in the U.S.? Will you meet them if you get the visa to the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: [Yes/No]. If yes: I have an aunt in [state], but my purpose is study. I may visit her during school breaks only.

27. Can you provide more details about your aunt's sponsorship case?

Suggested Answer: My aunt once applied for family sponsorship, but I am not part of that petition and this trip is only for study.

29. Why not wait for immigration before studying?

Suggested Answer: I don't have an immigration plan. My education and career goals can't wait for a long and uncertain process.
