About University of Toronto

The University of Toronto is Canada's leading institution of higher learning and research. Founded in 1827, it is consistently ranked as one of the top universities globally and is renowned for the excellence of its research, teaching, and innovation.

UofT offers world-class engineering programs with cutting-edge research facilities and strong industry connections. The Faculty of Applied Science & Engineering is home to nine departments offering undergraduate and graduate programs in various engineering disciplines including mechanical, electrical, computer, civil, and chemical engineering.

Located in downtown Toronto, students benefit from being in Canada's largest city and financial center. The university maintains strong partnerships with industry leaders and provides excellent opportunities for internships, co-op programs, and career development.

Available Scholarships

UofT Engineering Excellence Scholarship

Award: $10,000 per year (renewable)

Deadline: January 15, 2024

For incoming engineering students with outstanding academic achievement (95%+ average) and demonstrated leadership.

National Engineering Scholarship

Award: $5,000 (one-time)

Deadline: February 1, 2024

For students demonstrating financial need and academic merit (85%+ average) in any engineering discipline.

Women in Engineering Award

Award: $7,500 per year

Deadline: March 1, 2024

For female students entering any engineering program with a minimum 90% average and demonstrated interest in promoting women in STEM.

International Student Merit Scholarship

Award: $15,000 per year

Deadline: November 15, 2023

For exceptional international students entering first year of engineering with a minimum 90% equivalent average.

Admission Requirements

Academic Requirements

  • Ontario Secondary School Diploma (OSSD) or equivalent
  • Six Grade 12 U or M courses including English (ENG4U)
  • Advanced Functions (MHF4U) and Calculus & Vectors (MCV4U)
  • Chemistry (SCH4U) and Physics (SPH4U)
  • Minimum 85% average (competitive programs require 90%+)

International Students

  • TOEFL iBT: 100 (writing 22) or IELTS: 6.5 (no band below 6.0)
  • Country-specific academic requirements
  • Supplemental application for some programs
  • Financial documentation for study permit

Campus Life

The University of Toronto offers an exceptional campus experience with over 1,000 student organizations, world-class facilities, and a vibrant downtown Toronto location. Students can engage in research, athletics, arts, and community service while building lifelong connections.
