MOST FREQUENTLY USED VISA QUESTIONNAIRES FOR F1 VISA INTERVIEW

At the Student Visa Interview, The Consular Office often ask you many questions mainly within the following:

A. PERSONAL INFORMATION

3. How old are you? What's your job?

Suggested Answer: I am [your age] years old. I am currently a student preparing to study in the U.S. full-time.

4. What are your hobbies?

Suggested Answer: I enjoy reading, listening to music, and exploring new technology trends.

5. Do you like traveling? Have you ever been abroad?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I like traveling. I have visited [country/countries] for short trips. (If not yet: "Not yet, but I hope to travel more in the future.")

6. Have you ever lived away from your parents?

Suggested Answer: I lived in a dormitory during high school/college, so I am used to taking care of myself.

7. Do you have any friend? How many friends do you have?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I have many friends from school and extracurricular activities. I stay close to a small circle of about [number] good friends.

8. What do you often do in your free time?

Suggested Answer: I usually read books, watch educational videos, or play badminton with friends.

9. Do you like sports? Which kind of sport do you like best?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I like sports, especially badminton and swimming because they keep me active and relaxed.

12. Tell me something about your country!

Suggested Answer: Vietnam has a long history, beautiful landscapes like Ha Long Bay and the Mekong Delta, and a rich food culture with dishes such as pho and banh mi.

B. FAMILY BACKGROUND

1. What's your father's name? What's your mother's name?

Suggested Answer: My father's name is [Father's Name] and my mother's name is [Mother's Name].

2. Do you have any siblings? If you do, what are their names?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I have [number] sibling(s). [Brother/Sister]'s name is [Name(s)].

3. How old is your father/mother?

Suggested Answer: My father is [age] years old and my mother is [age] years old.

4. Are you living with your parents?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I currently live with my parents in [city]. (If not: "No, I live in a dorm/apartment near my school.")

5. Have your parents or your siblings traveled abroad?

Suggested Answer: Yes, they have visited [country/countries] for tourism. (If not: "No, they have not traveled abroad yet.")

6. Why don't your older brother(s)/sister(s) study abroad like you?

Suggested Answer: They chose to continue their education and career in Vietnam because it suits their goals and opportunities.

C. ACADEMIC BACKGROUND IN HOME COUNTRY (VIETNAM)

1. What grade are you studying in? What's your grade?

Suggested Answer: I am studying in Grade [number]. My overall grade is [e.g., "very good," "around 8.5 out of 10"].

2. What's your school name?

Suggested Answer: My school's name is [School Name].

3. What's something special about your school? Tell me something about your school.

Suggested Answer: My school is known for its strong science programs and friendly environment. We have modern labs and many clubs for students.

4. How many classes are there in your school?

Suggested Answer: There are about [number] classes in total.

5. What subjects are you good/bad at?

Suggested Answer: I'm good at [subject(s)], especially because I enjoy problem solving. I find [subject] more challenging, but I'm improving.

6. What are your favourite subjects? And why do you like them?

Suggested Answer: My favorite subjects are [subjects] because they help me develop critical thinking and match my future major.

7. What do you often do after school?

Suggested Answer: After school, I usually join a study group, play sports, or read books at home.

8. Who is your favourite teacher?

Suggested Answer: My favorite teacher is [Teacher's Name], who teaches [subject]. [He/She] makes lessons interesting and easy to understand.

9. What's your principal's name?

Suggested Answer: My principal's name is [Principal's Name].

D. STUDY PLAN IN THE USA

2. Why do you choose the U.S. to study but not Japan?

Suggested Answer: The U.S. offers flexible programs, strong research opportunities, and the exact major I want. Japan is excellent, but it doesn't match my academic goals as well.

5. What makes you choose this state to study?

Suggested Answer: The state has good universities, a safe community, and internship opportunities related to my field.

6. What school will you enroll in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I will enroll at [School Name].

7. What city will you arrive in? Tell me something about the city you will live in.

Suggested Answer: I will arrive in [City, State]. It's known for [brief facts—e.g., friendly community, technology industry, diverse culture].

8. Where is your school located? Why do you choose this state? What do you know about the state?

Suggested Answer: My school is in [City, State]. I chose this state because it offers strong programs in [field] and a safe, welcoming environment. The state is known for [a few facts—climate, industries, culture].

9. If you are provided a choice, where do you want to come?

Suggested Answer: I specifically chose [State/City] because it fits my education and lifestyle goals.

10. What is the address of your school?

Suggested Answer: The address is [Full School Address].

11. Tell me something about your school.

Suggested Answer: [School Name] is known for its excellent [program/major], supportive professors, and diverse student community.

12. Is your school a public or a private school?

Suggested Answer: It is a [public/private] school.

13. How can you know about this school?

Suggested Answer: I found it through online research, university rankings, and recommendations from my education consultant.

14. How can you get the I-20/DS-2019? How did you complete the admission form?

Suggested Answer: After receiving my acceptance letter, the school issued my I-20 once I submitted financial documents. I completed the admission form online through the university's application portal.

16. What grade will you study in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I will study as a [freshman / first-year undergraduate / grade 12 high school] student.

17. When will your school start? When is the upcoming intake of your school?

Suggested Answer: Classes begin on [start date]. The upcoming intake is the [Fall/Spring] semester of [year].

18. What subjects will you study at school?

Suggested Answer: I will study core courses like [examples: English composition, mathematics] and major-related classes in [field].

19. Is your school religious?

Suggested Answer: No, it is a non-religious institution. (If yes: "Yes, it has a Christian background but students of all beliefs are welcome.")

20. Do you have to study the Bible?

Suggested Answer: No, Bible study is not required. (If required: "Yes, there is one introductory religion course, which is part of the general education curriculum.")

21. Do you have to wear a uniform?

Suggested Answer: No, students can wear normal clothes, but some events may have a dress code.

26. What degree will you get after your graduation?

Suggested Answer: I will receive a [Bachelor's/Master's] degree in [major].

29. Have you bought insurance?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I purchased the required student health insurance through the school's plan.

30. Have you paid the program fee? By what way?

Suggested Answer: Yes, the program fee was paid via [bank transfer/credit card/online payment] as instructed by the school.

31. Where will you live in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I will live in [on-campus housing / an apartment near campus / a host family's home].

32. With whom will you stay in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I will stay [alone in student housing / with a host family].

33. Tell me something about your host family.

Suggested Answer: My host family consists of [brief description: e.g., a couple with two children], and they are experienced in hosting international students.

34. Who will pick you up at the airport upon your arrival?

Suggested Answer: A representative from the school/host family will pick me up at the airport and take me to my accommodation.
