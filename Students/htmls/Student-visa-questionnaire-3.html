FINANCIAL BACKGROUND & OTHER QUESTIONS

Please answer the following questions to prepare for your visa interview.

E. FINANCIAL BACKGROUND QUESTIONS

4. How can your parents pay for your study?

Suggested Answer: My parents have stable jobs and savings. They will use their salary, long-term savings, and a dedicated education fund to cover my tuition and living costs.

6. How many houses or lands do your parents have?

Suggested Answer: They own our family house and an additional piece of land in [city/province].

7. Do your parents have a car?

Suggested Answer: Yes, they own one family car for daily use.

8. How much will your parents give you a month when you live in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: They will send me about [exact amount in USD] per month to cover living expenses.

If your parents are currently working for a specific organization:

1. Whom does your father/mother work for?

Suggested Answer: My father works for [Company Name], and my mother works for [Company Name].

2. For how long have your parents worked for this company?

Suggested Answer: My father has worked there for [X] years and my mother for [Y] years.

3. Do you know where your father/mother's company is? What's the address?

Suggested Answer: Yes, my father's company is located at [Full Address]. My mother's company is at [Full Address].

4. What are your father/mother's company products?

Suggested Answer: My father's company produces [main products/services]. My mother's company specializes in [main products/services].

5. Do you know any of your parents' colleagues? Who is he/she?

Suggested Answer: Yes, I know [Name], who works with my father/mother in the same department.

G. QUESTIONS TO TEST YOUR REACTION AGAINST A TRICKY QUESTION

4. Have you ever lived away from your parents? What will you do if you miss them?

Suggested Answer: I have lived away during short school trips. If I miss them, I'll video call often and stay focused on my studies to keep myself busy.

5. Do you have any friend in the U.S.? If you do, tell me something about him/her.

Suggested Answer: I have a friend named [Name] who is studying in [City/State]. We occasionally chat online, and they share useful tips about student life in the U.S.

6. What difficulties do you think you may encounter in the U.S.?

Suggested Answer: I might face cultural differences and homesickness at first, but I'm ready to adapt, join student clubs, and improve my English to overcome those challenges.

7. What is the most memorable event in your life?

Suggested Answer: One of my most memorable events was [example: winning a national science competition / graduating high school], because it showed me the value of hard work.

8. What will you do if your parents run out of money and cannot afford your study?

Suggested Answer: I will look for on-campus part-time work allowed by my visa, apply for scholarships, and carefully manage my expenses so I can continue my education.
